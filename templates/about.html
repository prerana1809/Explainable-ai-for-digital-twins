About the Project

What is Digital Twins?

Digital Twins are virtual replicas of physical systems or devices that allow us to monitor, analyze, and predict their behavior in real-time. In the context of electric vehicles, digital twins are used to create a digital counterpart of the battery, providing insights into its performance, degradation, and potential failures.

What are SoC and SoH?

State of Charge (SoC): Represents the current charge level of a battery as a percentage of its maximum capacity.
State of Health (SoH): Measures the overall health and remaining lifespan of a battery compared to its original state.

What is Deep Learning and Explainable AI?

Deep Learning: An advanced subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with multiple layers to model and predict complex patterns.

  • DNN (Deep Neural Networks): Composed of multiple layers of neurons, DNNs are used for tasks like classification and regression.
  • LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory): A specialized type of neural network designed for sequential data, such as time-series data.

Explainable AI (XAI): Refers to techniques that make AI models interpretable and understandable to humans. Surrogate models, such as Decision Trees and Linear Regression, can be used to approximate and explain the predictions made by complex AI models.

Project Goals and Aims

This project focuses on developing a system to predict the State of Charge (SoC) and State of Health (SoH) of electric vehicle batteries. By leveraging digital twins, advanced deep learning models (DNN and LSTM), and explainable AI techniques, the aim is to provide accurate and interpretable predictions to enhance battery management systems. The ultimate goal is to improve battery efficiency, safety, and lifespan in electric vehicles.
